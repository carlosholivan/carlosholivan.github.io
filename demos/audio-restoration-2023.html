VOCAL RESTORATION VIA SAMPLING PRE-TRAINED DIFFUSION MODELS
WITH MULTIPLE GUIDANCE

Carlos Hernandez-Olivan*, †, Koichi Saito†, Naoki Murata†, Chieh-Hsin Lai†, Marco A. Rammirez-Martinez†, Wei-Hsing Liao†, Yuki Mitsufuji†

*Dept. of Electronic Engineering and Communications, University of Zaragoza, Spain
†Sony Research, Tokyo, Japan

Abstract

Restoring degraded music signals is essential to enhance audio quality for downstream music manipulation. While diffusion-based music restoration methods have demonstrated impressive performance against problem-oriented models, the effectiveness of different guidance techniques in music restoration remains underexplored. This paper introduces diverse diffusion guidance methods for music restoration tasks, including time-traveling technique and pseudo-inverse guidance ($\Pi$GDM). We trained two models: Constant Q-Transform (CQT) and waveform using various types of vocal data. The waveform model yields comparable performance to the CQT model, boasting an 18x reduction in inference time. We validated our methods for declipping and bandwidth extension tasks. In declipping task, the addition of time-traveling guidance outperforms prior approaches for various levels of distortion. Furthermore, $\Pi$GDM performs similarly to existing techniques for bandwidth extension task.
