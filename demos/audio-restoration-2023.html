MUSIC RESTORATION VIA SAMPLING PRE-TRAINED DIFFUSION MODELS
WITH MULTIPLE GUIDANCE

Carlos Hernandez-Olivan*, †, Koichi Saito†, Naoki Murata†, Chieh-Hsin Lai†, Marco A. Rammirez-Martinez†, Wei-Hsing Liao†, Yuki Mitsufuji†

*Dept. of Electronic Engineering and Communications, University of Zaragoza, Spain
†Sony Research, Tokyo, Japan

Abstract

To restore the degraded music signal is a necessary technique to clean up audio for downstream music manipulations. Diffusion-based music restoration methods have shown impressive performance. In this paper, we extend previous diffusion-based works on music restoration for vocal data. We compare different sampling methods for two models that take a Constant Q-Transform (CQT) and a waveform as their inputs for the declipping and bandwidth extension tasks. We demonstrate how for the declipping task, adding time-traveling to the sampling outperforms previous works for both dry and wet vocals for 5dB and 10dB. On the other hand, a problem-agnostic pseudo-inverse guidance ($\Pi$GDM) performs similar than existing sampling techniques in the audio domain for bandwidth extension. Also, the waveform model performance is close to the CQT model's performance with a 18x reduction factor in inference time.
